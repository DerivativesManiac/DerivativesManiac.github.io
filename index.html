  3. Discretionary Option Trading 1.0 (Live Trading: 2017 - 2018)

    I started trading equity options at a prop shop based in HK after I left academia in 2016H2. I developed a discretionary option trading strategy, i.e., long pure calls if I believe the underlying stock will rise to a certain level within a certain period of time. Key characteristcs include:

    • Total return: 500%
    • Maximum drawdown: 72%
    • Instruments: Long Call, Long Vol on US stocks
    • Holding period: Weeks
    • Portfolio size: 20-30
    • Maintain Long Vol positions to protect the portfolio against market crash

    From the chart on the right, you may notice that the accumulative P&L is positively correlated with S&P Index. This is because the main component of the portfolio was long calls. In addition, Long Vol positions did protect the portfolio at the beginning of 2018 when the market crashed. Below are several key opportunities that I captured during the trading period of 2017-2018:

    • Energy (OIL US): I closely followed a fundamental investor called HFIR on Seeking Alpha in 2017-2018. He is an expert in Crude Oil research. Based on supply/demand data provided by him and my own quantitative signals, I made a home run in 2018Q1 by long OIL calls.
    • Biotech (XBI US): Immuno-oncology, a game changing technique to cure blood cancer, was hot in 2017-2018. I bought 30 biotech stocks that involved Immuno-oncology therapies. 3 of them got accquired during 2017-2018. They were Kite Pharma, Juno Therapeutics, and Ablynx NV. My little Kite even ranked 7th in the list of 50 smartest companies selected by MIT Tech Review in 2017.
    • Retail (XRT US): Retail sector plunged by about 20% in 2017H1 due to the consensus belief that online shoppoing (i.e. Amazon) would destroy the retail business. While I believed that Amazon would have negative impact on department stores such as Macy's, Target, JCPenney, and etc., it would boost the revenue of retail companies who have their own brands such as Ralph Lauren, Gap, Nike, and etc. at the same time. The beauty of XRT US is that it is a price-weighted ETF and only a few names among over 100 constituents are department stores. Therefore it was no-brainer for me to trade long Vega on XRT US back then, expecting the correlation among constituents would go up when the consensus turns around.
